bce-simulation

bce-simulation is a set of simulations written for a research project regarding Buffon's Coin Experiment and a couple of its variants. It serves to verify theoretical findings through experimental methods.

Simulation

The Coin Simulation generates a graph of Buffon's Coin Experiment, showing the probability of a coin touching one of the lines of a square grid.

The Coin Simulation (A variation) generates a graph of Buffon's Coin Experiment with additional calculations: it detects if the coin can really balance with additional calculations, and shows the probability of the coin touching one of the lines of a square grid.

The Needle Simulation generates a graph of Buffon's Needle Experiment: it shows the probability of a needle touching one of the lines of two parallel lines.

Type of Graph

Increasing width of square gap generates a graph plotting width of square gap against probability of coin touching the square grid.

Increasing radius generates a graph plotting radius of coin against probability of coin touching the square grid.

Increasing width of square gap generates a graph plotting width of square gap against probability of coin balancing on the square grid.

Increasing radius generates a graph plotting radius of coin against probability of coin balancing on the square grid.

Increasing gap width generates a graph plotting width of gap against probability of needle touching at least one of the lines.

Increasing length generates a graph plotting length of needle against probability of needle touching at least one of the lines.

Number of trials to run

The number of trials to run is the number of times the simulation will be run. The greater the number of trials, the greater the accuracy of results.

Number of steps to take

The number of steps to take specifies the number of x-axis values.
